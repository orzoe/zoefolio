Optimizing In-Store Offer for Mobile

After we launched the first version of TrialPay's In-Store Offer Program with responsive design, I wanted to further opitmize the mobile experience. I proposed a new design with interactive prototype.

Fully functioning web app with people's real finance data.
Charts and graphs with real data, built with d3/html/css.
"Sitemap" for Beta.
Rough sketches for onboarding experience.
Specs for various states of a calendar event and its detail popover.

I joined Gleam when it was only a few months old. It just started raising seed money and its product vision was in incubation. The high-level promise is a comprehensive personal finance management service that will bring you peace of mind and save you money in the long run.

The founding team consisted of 1 founder, 1 chief marketing officer, 2 engineers, and me as the designer. Besides raising money, our top priority was to produce a closed beta as soon as possible so we can validate our concepts with real users and their real financial data.

I started by interviewing prospective users and evaluating exisiting products/services out there (e.g. Quicken, Mint, Pocketsmith, HelloWallet, Check, Manila, etc.). Meanwhile, I worked side-by-side with the engineers to design and build out an ambitiously wide range of features. These include calendar, to-do list, bill payment, transaction aggregation, secured message, notification, document management, data visualization, etc. Most of the time, instead of writing out detailed specs, I either coded up prototypes that can later be turned into production code or directly implemented the front-end in order to speed up the turn-around time.

At the end of my 5 months there, we had a clear direction for the product beta and a fully working web app but still no funding...
